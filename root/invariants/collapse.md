# collapse.md
*(Tower‑side invariant document)*

## 1. Role of Collapse
Collapse is the **Tower‑only process** that reduces a valid single spine into a stable, unambiguous **shape**.

Collapse is:

- structural  
- mechanical  
- deterministic  
- Tower‑internal  

Collapse is **not**:

- semantic interpretation  
- meaning extraction  
- system routing  
- pipeline selection  
- geometry or movement  

Collapse happens **before** any wrapper‑tag is produced.  
Collapse never crosses the membrane.

---

## 2. Collapse Preconditions
Collapse can only occur when:

- exactly one spine is present  
- the spine is structurally valid  
- the turn is unambiguous  
- no fused or entangled operations exist  
- no cross‑turn references are required  
- no implicit meaning is needed  

If any precondition fails:

- collapse does not occur  
- no shape is produced  
- no wrapper‑tag is emitted  
- the system does not activate  

---

## 3. Collapse Inputs
Collapse receives:

- a single extracted spine  
- Tower‑side structural context (if any)  

Collapse does **not** receive:

- content  
- meaning  
- system state  
- registry information  
- semantics information  
- engine information  

Collapse is **Tower‑only**.

---

## 4. Collapse Outputs
Collapse produces:

- a **shape** (Tower‑internal)  
- or **no shape** (rejection)  

Collapse never produces:

- content  
- wrapper‑tags  
- registry‑tags  
- pipeline structures  
- coordinates  
- engine objects  

Shapes remain in the Tower.  
Only the wrapper‑tag (derived later) crosses the membrane.

---

## 5. Collapse Rules
Collapse must:

- reduce the spine to its minimal structural form  
- remove all ambiguity  
- remove all optional or implied meaning  
- enforce Tower boundaries  
- enforce single‑intent structure  
- enforce single‑operation structure  
- enforce single‑spine structure  

Collapse must **never**:

- infer meaning  
- guess intent  
- merge unrelated operations  
- preserve ambiguity  
- preserve fused structures  

Collapse is a **purification step**, not an interpretive one.

---

## 6. Collapse Failure Conditions
Collapse must fail (and produce no shape) when:

- the spine contains multiple independent operations  
- the spine contains fused or entangled operations  
- the spine requires implicit meaning  
- the spine references previous turns  
- the spine violates Tower boundaries  
- the spine is malformed or contradictory  
- the spine cannot be reduced deterministically  

On collapse failure:

- no shape is produced  
- no wrapper‑tag is produced  
- the system does not activate  

---

## 7. Collapse and Ambiguity
Ambiguity must be resolved **before** collapse.

Rules:

- If ambiguity is resolvable → clarify → collapse  
- If ambiguity is not resolvable → reject → no collapse  

Collapse must never encode unresolved ambiguity.  
Collapse must never guess.

---

## 8. Collapse and Boundaries
Collapse enforces Tower boundaries:

- no cross‑turn references  
- no multi‑intent turns  
- no fused shapes  
- no hidden structure  
- no semantic leakage  

If a boundary is violated:

- collapse fails  
- no shape is produced  
- the system remains inactive  

---

## 9. Collapse → Shape → Wrapper‑Tag
Collapse produces a **shape**.  
The wrapper then maps the shape to a **wrapper‑tag**.

Important:

- collapse does not produce wrapper‑tags  
- collapse does not know wrapper‑tags  
- collapse does not know semantics  
- collapse does not know registry  
- collapse does not know the system  

Collapse is **upstream** of all system behavior.

---

## 10. Turn Isolation
Collapse is **per‑turn only**:

- collapse does not reuse previous shapes  
- collapse does not reuse previous spines  
- collapse does not reuse previous ambiguity states  
- collapse does not reuse previous collapse results  

Each turn is a fresh collapse event.

---

## 11. Collapse Membrane Guarantee
Collapse:

- exists only in the Tower  
- never crosses the membrane  
- never appears in semantics  
- never appears in registry  
- never appears in concierge  
- never appears in the engine  

Collapse is a Tower‑internal purification step.  
It ensures that only **clean, clarified, single‑intent shapes** can produce wrapper‑tags.

Nothing fuses.
