# System Invariants

System invariants define the non‑negotiable physics of the system.  
They are upstream of all domains, all rules, all engines, and all execution.

They cannot be changed, interpreted, or overridden.

---

## 1. Identity Invariant
The system has a single identity that:
- does not drift  
- does not split  
- does not fuse  
- does not invert  

Identity cannot be renamed, reinterpreted, or reassigned.

---

## 2. Boundary Invariant
All boundaries must remain:
- intact  
- explicit  
- non‑porous  
- non‑semantic  

No domain may cross, fuse, or reinterpret boundaries.

---

## 3. Separation Invariant
The following separations must always hold:
- structure ≠ engine  
- engine ≠ registry  
- registry ≠ semantics  
- semantics ≠ content  
- geometry ≠ placement  

No domain may become another.

---

## 4. Directionality Invariant
The following directionalities must remain stable:
- naming → structure (never reversed)  
- structure → geometry (never reversed)  
- registry → execution (never reversed)  
- invariants → engine (never reversed)  

No downstream domain may influence upstream physics.

---

## 5. Non‑Interpretation Invariant
The system must not:
- interpret meaning  
- infer intent  
- assign semantics  
- reinterpret naming  

All operations are structural, not semantic.

---

## 6. Non‑Mutation Invariant
The system must not mutate:
- identity  
- boundaries  
- categories  
- geometry  
- naming rules  

Execution cannot alter system physics.

---

## 7. Reversibility Invariant
All system operations must be:
- reversible  
- residue‑free  
- non‑mutative  

No operation may leave structural or semantic residue.

---

## 8. Containment Invariant
All domains must remain:
- local  
- bounded  
- isolated  

No domain may leak into another.

---

## 9. Drift Invariant
The system must prevent:
- category drift  
- naming drift  
- adjacency drift  
- placement drift  
- semantic drift  

Drift is always a violation.

---

## 10. Collapse Invariant
Collapse must remain:
- detectable  
- isolated  
- non‑propagating  
- non‑semantic  

Collapse is a failure state, not a mechanism.

---

## 11. Error Invariant
Errors must remain:
- structural  
- local  
- reversible  
- non‑semantic  

Errors cannot propagate or reinterpret meaning.

---

## 12. Execution Invariant
Execution must:
- respect invariants  
- respect boundaries  
- respect geometry  
- respect separation  

Execution cannot override system physics.

---

## 13. Registry Invariant
The registry must:
- remain structural  
- remain non‑semantic  
- remain non‑interpretive  

The registry cannot assign meaning or identity.

---

## 14. Naming Invariant
Naming must:
- remain structural  
- remain non‑semantic  
- remain non‑interpretive  

Naming cannot become meaning.

---

## 15. Stability Invariant
The system must remain:
- stable across time  
- stable across operations  
- stable across domains  

Stability is a non‑negotiable property.
