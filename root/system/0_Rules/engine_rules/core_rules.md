# Core Engine Rules

The engine is a mechanical layer.  
It does not interpret, evaluate, or assign meaning.  
It moves content only by following coordinates provided from upstream.

## 1. The engine moves on coordinates
The engine receives a coordinate sequence from upstream.  
It does not know how the coordinates were chosen.  
It does not interpret or evaluate them.  
It follows the sequence mechanically.

## 2. The object is positional, not semantic
The object carried by the engine is placed into a position within a structural carrier.  
Its position determines its movement.  
Meaning plays no role.

## 3. The engine does not bind content by understanding
The engine performs no semantic analysis.  
It does not interpret, evaluate, or understand the content it carries.

## 4. The engine binds content by placement
Content is attached mechanically to the structural carrier.  
Placement determines movement.  
No semantic binding occurs.

## 5. The engine holds content only for the duration of its mechanical operation
Content exists only while the engine is actively processing it.  
No implicit state is retained.

## 6. The engine discards content when its mechanical operation completes
All content is removed when the engine finishes its movement.  
Nothing persists.

## 7. The engine cannot reuse or leak content
No content from previous operations is accessible.  
No content can be carried forward.

## 8. Nothing fuses
No domain, content, or structure merges with another.  
The engine remains isolated, mechanical, and non-semantic.
