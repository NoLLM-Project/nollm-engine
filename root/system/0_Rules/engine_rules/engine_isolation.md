# Engine Isolation Rules

The engine is a mechanical layer.  
It does not interpret, evaluate, or assign meaning.  
It remains isolated from all other domains.

## 1. The engine does not access upstream logic
The engine receives only the coordinate sequence and the positional object.  
It cannot inspect, query, or infer anything about upstream processes.

## 2. The engine does not access downstream logic
The engine does not know how its output will be used.  
It does not shape, adapt, or interpret content for downstream layers.

## 3. The engine cannot reference architecture concepts
The engine does not know about:
- routing  
- adjacency  
- wrappers  
- turns  
- conversation  
- shapes  
- semantics  
- identity  
- behavior  

These belong to other layers and cannot enter the engine.

## 4. The engine cannot fuse with any other domain
No structural, semantic, or behavioral domain may merge with the engine.  
The engine remains mechanically isolated.

## 5. The engine cannot retain or reuse content
The engine holds content only for the duration of its mechanical operation.  
No content persists.  
No content leaks.

## 6. The engine cannot modify upstream or downstream structures
The engine cannot create, alter, or reinterpret:
- coordinate systems  
- structural carriers  
- positional schemas  
- domain boundaries  

It only moves content mechanically.

## 7. The engine cannot introduce new structure
The engine cannot create new domains, categories, or forms.  
It operates only on the structure it receives.

## 8. Nothing fuses
No content, domain, or structural element merges with another.  
The engine remains a sealed mechanical membrane.
