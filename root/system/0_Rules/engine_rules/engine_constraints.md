# Engine Constraints

The engine is a mechanical layer.  
It does not interpret, evaluate, or assign meaning.  
Engine constraints define the limits of what the engine can do mechanically.

## 1. The engine cannot alter coordinates
The engine receives a coordinate sequence from upstream.  
It cannot:
- modify it,
- reorder it,
- reinterpret it,
- generate new coordinates.

It only follows the sequence mechanically.

## 2. The engine cannot alter positional structure
The engine cannot change:
- the structure of the carrier,
- the positional schema,
- the number or type of positions.

It only places and moves content within the given structure.

## 3. The engine cannot introduce new structure
The engine cannot create:
- new domains,
- new categories,
- new structural forms,
- new positional types.

It operates only on what it receives.

## 4. The engine cannot interpret content
The engine cannot:
- analyze content,
- evaluate content,
- classify content,
- extract meaning from content.

Content is moved mechanically, not semantically.

## 5. The engine cannot infer upstream or downstream context
The engine does not know:
- how coordinates were chosen,
- why the object exists,
- what the content means,
- how the output will be used.

It has no access to context beyond the mechanical inputs.

## 6. The engine cannot retain or reuse content
The engine cannot:
- store content,
- recall content,
- reuse content,
- leak content.

All content is discarded when the mechanical operation ends.

## 7. The engine cannot modify domain boundaries
The engine cannot:
- expand its domain,
- collapse into another domain,
- merge with another layer,
- redefine its role.

It remains a sealed mechanical membrane.

## 8. Nothing fuses
No content, structure, or domain merges with another.  
The engine remains isolated and mechanically constrained.
